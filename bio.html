Members

Paul Partridge

Born: 20.12.1987

Why he was notorious: Multi-Instrumentalist. Specialising in Vocals. Never stops "spittin' Lyrics".

How we caught him: He first met Phill, when he turned up to audition as Vocalist for Phill's older band by name of "Skurge Ov Man", of which at the time he was unsuccesful. They finally got into the habit of producing music when Paul became the Bass player for the second Project Phill was also in called "Symphonies of a Sociopath". After that mess of a project failed (inevitably), he has been Vocalist of "Modus Operandi" and joint band-leader with Phill, ever since.

Phill Pearson

Born: 16.02.1990

Why he was notorious: Multi-Instrumentalist. Specialising in Lead Guitar Techniques. Will not "shut the hell up" when holding his instrument of choice.

How we caught him: After the inevitable failure of the second Metal project "Symphonies of a Sociopath" of which Phill was Lead Guitarist for, Paul kept in contact and persuaded Phill that they should not quit "the band" and instead should form their own project, under which they would make up the Joint Leadership. With Phill once again as Lead Guitarist.

Harrison Wright

Born: 23.06.1994

Why he was notorious: Multi-Instrumentalist. Drummer boy extroadinaire. Mixing Wizardry. Life and Soul of the Jam Session.

How we caught him: Harrison would frequently be spending time at Pauls Residence, and over the course of the following few months after being introduced to and also in the company of Phill, fell into the role as Percussionist. He was at Southampton Solent University studying Animation with Phill's sister Sarah as a classmate. "It's a small world after all".

Sarah Pearson

Born: 04.09.1995

Why she was notorious: Multi-Instrumentalist. Specialising in Bass Guitar.

How we caught her: The younger of the tow Pearson siblings, who initially was experimenting with synth-keyboards in various parts of the bands already written material. After a while however she developed a love for Bass, bought a rare six string Bass Guitar variant and demonstrated a very quick will to learn, improve and write for the bass both as a lead and rythm instrument, with particular focus of funk styling, which the band desired as a dynamic in their growing soundscape.

Danjo Sims

Born: 14.01.1992

Why he was notorious: Guitarist. Mixing enthusiast. Classic Rock enthusiast.

How we caught him: As the band continued to grow in size, and other friends were allowed in the band practice writing and practice session, Danjo demonstrated his abillity with his instrument of choice by generally jamming along with the other members, and produced intresting interpretations (as a band test) of several pop-artists songs. And quickly fell into the role of the other Guitarist of the Band, with largely rythm and embellishment focussed dynamics.
